Milk
A white liquid produced by mammals, primarily cows, that is rich in nutrients such as calcium, protein, and vitamins. It is commonly consumed as a beverage and used as an ingredient in a wide variety of foods, including cheese, yogurt, butter, and many baked goods.
Bread
A staple food made from flour, water, and usually yeast or another leavening agent, baked into loaves or other shapes. Bread is a fundamental part of many diets around the world and comes in a wide variety of types, such as white, whole wheat, multigrain, sourdough, and rye. It can be eaten on its own, used for sandwiches, or served as a side with meals.
Fruits
Sweet and fleshy products of plants that are typically consumed raw and are rich in vitamins, minerals, and fiber. Common examples include apples, bananas, oranges, and berries.
